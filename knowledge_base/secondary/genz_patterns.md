# Gen Z Financial Behavior Patterns (Germany)

## Income Reality (Age 17–23)

- Typical student job income: €520–€900/month (Minijob or part-time)
- Ausbildung salary: €800–€1,200/month
- First full-time job after Ausbildung/degree: €1,300–€2,000 net
- Many still receive partial parental support
- High rent share relative to income in major cities (Berlin, Munich, Hamburg)

## Spending Patterns

- Rent (WG room): €350–€700 depending on city
- Subscriptions: €30–€80/month (Spotify, Netflix, Gym, iCloud, etc.)
- Food delivery + eating out: €150–€300/month
- Clubbing/festivals: high seasonal spikes
- Tech purchases often financed or delayed-savings

## Financial Anxiety & Mindset

- Many feel “behind” financially compared to social media peers
- Low confidence in investing knowledge
- Fear of market crashes
- Simultaneous desire for financial independence and lifestyle freedom
- High emotional stress related to inflation and rising rent

## Digital Financial Behavior

- High usage of neobrokers (Trade Republic, Scalable Capital)
- Finance education via TikTok, Instagram, YouTube
- Distrust of traditional banks
- Influenced by finance influencers but skeptical of “get rich quick” content

## Key Tensions

- “I want to invest” vs “I have no money left”
- “Everyone travels” vs saving for long term
- Payday optimism vs end-of-month stress
- ETF curiosity but no clear plan
