# Competitor Content Style Analysis – Guardrails for MoneySavvy AI

Purpose:
This document defines common shallow finance content patterns to avoid.
It does NOT provide external research. It defines stylistic differentiation rules.

---

## Common Shallow Patterns in Gen Z Finance Content

### 1. Generic Budget Advice

Examples of shallow framing:
- “Track your expenses.”
- “Stop buying coffee.”
- “Save 20% of your income.”
- “Cut unnecessary subscriptions.”

Problem:
- Not income-specific
- No realistic rent burden acknowledgment
- No Western European nuance
- Feels recycled

---

### 2. Hustle / Shame-Based Tone

Examples:
- “If you're broke it's your fault.”
- “You’re lazy if you don’t invest.”
- “Grind harder.”

Problem:
- Ignores entry-level income realities
- Alienates audience
- Not aligned with supportive tone

---

### 3. Crypto or High-Risk Hype

Examples:
- “This coin will 10x.”
- “You’re missing out.”

Problem:
- Encourages unstable financial behavior
- Not aligned with long-term wealth building

---

### 4. Aesthetic Minimalism Without Depth

Examples:
- “Financial freedom is a mindset.”
- “Your future self will thank you.”
- No numbers, no scenario.

Problem:
- Inspirational but not actionable
- No practical guidance

---

## Cliché Phrases to Avoid

- Financial freedom journey
- Multiple income streams
- Passive income hack
- Mindset shift
- Stop wasting money on coffee
- Build generational wealth at 20

---

## MoneySavvy Differentiation Principles

Content MUST:

- Use realistic € income examples
- Acknowledge rent burden
- Reflect emotional tension
- Avoid shame-based tone
- Include at least one concrete number
- Offer one small, actionable next step
- Sound like a supportive older sibling



Content Usage Guidance:

When generating content:
- Use the numbers provided here.
- Do not introduce US-based salary examples.
- Do not invent additional statistics.
- Keep examples aligned with entry-level income ranges listed above.
